**United Laboratories — Project File**  
**Designation:** Project: Apis Nexus
**Category:** Hybridization — Swarm Coordination & Recon Unit  
**Status:** Active Testing

---

### **Overview**

Project: Apis Nexus transforms hosts into insectoid-human hybrids with traits inspired by bees. These hybrids are intended for **swarm-based operations, reconnaissance, and tactical coordination**. The project emphasizes cooperative behavior, hive-minded instincts, and environmental adaptability, enabling hosts to act individually or as part of a networked swarm.

---

### **Vector & Delivery**

- **Vector:** Viral-genetic construct integrating insect DNA sequences, optimized for flight, sensory adaptation, and communication.
- **Delivery:** Injection, aerosolized spores, or spire-mediated exposure.
- **Latency:** 4–12 hours for initial transformation.

---

### **Transformation Stages**
**Stage I (0–4 hours):**
- Subtle **exoskeletal growths** appear on limbs and shoulders.
- Enhanced **vision**, including ability to detect UV patterns and motion sensitivity.
- Host begins shrinking slightly to improve agility.

**Stage II (4–8 hours):**
- **Wings emerge** (functional but fragile initially), allowing short bursts of flight.
- Hands and feet develop **grasping adaptations** for climbing and hive-like structures.
- Neural patterns shift to **enhance cooperative instincts** with other hosts or swarm-linked AI.

**Stage III (8–24 hours):**

- Full Apis Nexus form realized:
    - **Flight-capable wings**, chitinous segments for protection.
    - Enhanced **pheromone signaling** for communication within groups.
    - Slight size reduction compared to normal human size for stealth and maneuverability.
    - High-speed reflexes and synchronized behavior with other Apis Nexus units.

---

### **Tactical Applications**
- **Swarm Recon:** Can infiltrate and scout multiple targets simultaneously, coordinated via hive-mind neural links.    
- **Espionage:** Capable of silent observation, rapid reporting, and infiltration in groups.
- **Sabotage:** Can interact with mechanical systems or deploy biological payloads covertly.
- **Hive Operations:** Groups operate instinctively, forming highly efficient tactical units with rapid reaction times.

---

### **Control & Containment**
- Neural implants allow UL remote guidance and override via **Project: Override Spear** or **Omega Prime clearance**.
- Must avoid prolonged exposure to extreme cold or high-speed winds, which can damage wings.    
- Nutrition: High-energy intake needed to maintain wing function and metabolic demands.

---

### **Psychological Notes**
- Hive-minded instincts can cause **over-reliance on group behavior**, making solo operations more difficult initially.
- Hosts retain **full cognitive abilities**, but pheromone-driven impulses may subtly influence decision-making.
- Training required to balance individual strategy with swarm coordination.
