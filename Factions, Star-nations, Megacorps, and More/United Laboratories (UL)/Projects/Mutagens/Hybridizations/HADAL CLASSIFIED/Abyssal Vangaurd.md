**United Laboratories — Project File**  
**Designation:** Project: Abyssal Vanguard  
**Category:** Hybridization — Aquatic Combat & Loyalty Bond  
**Status:** Experimental Deployment

---

### **Overview**

Project: Abyssal Vanguard transforms terrestrial hosts into highly capable aquatic hybrids with partial predatory and defensive adaptations. This mutagen is designed to improve survival, combat capability, and cooperative function with a designated controlling subject (Sebastian). It introduces subtle morphological and physiological changes, granting aquatic versatility while retaining human cognitive capacity.

---

### **Vector & Delivery**

- **Vector:** Viral-genetic construct integrating traits from deep-sea predators, coral-dwelling organisms, and cephalopod reflex systems.
    
- **Delivery:** Intravenous injection or aerosolized microcapsules targeted at bloodstream absorption.
    
- **Latency:** 4–10 hours for initial physical adaptations; neural integration and loyalty bonding stabilize over 12–18 hours.
    

---

### **Transformation Stages**

**Stage I (0–4 hours):**

- Host experiences minor dermal thickening and water-resistant adaptations.
    
- Initial neurological integration begins; host begins responding instinctively to aquatic environmental stimuli.
    
- Reflexive enhancement improves balance, coordination, and fine motor control.
    

**Stage II (4–8 hours):**

- Formation of subtle webbing between fingers and toes, increasing swimming efficiency.
    
- Development of shallow gill slits along neck, allowing intermittent underwater respiration.
    
- Muscle fiber composition adjusts for aquatic motion while maintaining terrestrial functionality.
    

**Stage III (8–12 hours):**

- Minor tail-like structure emerges from coccyx; flexible, capable of balance and limited propulsion.
    
- Eyes adapt for low-light aquatic environments, improving contrast and motion detection underwater.
    
- Bioluminescent patterning appears along the forearms and spine; triggers include environmental light levels and proximity to controlling subject.
    

**Stage IV (12–18 hours):**

- Neural bonding pathways finalize; host demonstrates instinctive alignment with controlling subject.
    
- Physiological adaptations stabilize, providing both underwater and surface mobility.
    
- Cognitive function remains intact, but reflexive cooperation with controlling subject is reinforced.
    

---

### **Tactical & Practical Applications**

- **Amphibious Operations:** Host capable of swimming, diving, and limited underwater combat.
    
- **Team Coordination:** Instinctive response to controlling subject increases efficiency during high-risk or tactical maneuvers.
    
- **Environmental Adaptation:** Can navigate and monitor aquatic environments while remaining effective on land.
    
- **Defense & Rescue:** Tail and webbing assist in underwater stabilization, object manipulation, and self-defense.
    

---

### **Control & Containment**

- Neural pathways allow controlling subject to influence instincts, but host retains autonomy and judgment.
    
- Requires aquatic-friendly environments for prolonged operations; standard terrestrial training recommended.
    
- Diet and hydration must support increased metabolic demands due to hybrid physiology.
    

---

### **Psychological Notes**

- Host may initially feel disorientation, claustrophobia, or mild anxiety from gill formation and tail emergence.
    
- Bonding with controlling subject provides psychological reassurance; instinctive protective behaviors develop toward allies.
    
- Training for both aquatic and terrestrial coordination is recommended to prevent maladaptive behaviors.
