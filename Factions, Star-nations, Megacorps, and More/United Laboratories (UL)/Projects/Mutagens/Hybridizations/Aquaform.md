**United Laboratories — Project File**  
**Designation:** Project: Aquaform
**Category:** Hybridization — Aquatic Adaptation & Exploration  
**Status:** Active Testing / Public Release

---

### **Overview**

Project: Aquaform transforms subjects into highly capable amphibious hybrids. Hosts retain human cognitive function but develop physical traits optimized for underwater exploration, survival, and efficiency. Adaptations include enhanced swimming ability, underwater respiration, and sensory enhancement for aquatic environments.

---

### **Vector & Delivery**

- **Vector:** Viral-genetic construct incorporating traits from aquatic vertebrates (sharks, cephalopods, amphibians).
    
- **Delivery:** Intravenous injection, aerosolized microcapsules, or immersion in transformation bath.
    
- **Latency:** 3–8 hours for initial transformation.
    

---

### **Transformation Stages**

**Stage I (0–2 hours):**

- Gills begin forming along the neck and rib cage.
    
- Minor modifications to lungs for dual air/water breathing.
    
- Skin becomes slightly more hydrodynamic and water-resistant.
    

**Stage II (2–5 hours):**

- Tail fin emerges from coccyx, extending downward for propulsion.
    
- Webbing develops between fingers and toes.
    
- Musculature adjusts for efficient swimming; joints slightly realign for aquatic motion.
    

**Stage III (5–8 hours):**

- Full gill function established, allowing indefinite underwater respiration.
    
- Bioluminescent patterns appear along the spine and limbs for communication and signaling in deep water.
    
- Eyes adapt for low-light underwater vision, retaining land-based vision.
    
- Skin secretion of hydrophilic compounds reduces drag and enhances healing in water.
    

---

### **Tactical & Practical Applications**

- **Exploration:** Perfect for underwater research, salvage operations, and mapping aquatic ecosystems.
    
- **Rescue Operations:** Capable of rapid underwater rescue or transport of humans and supplies.
    
- **Environmental Monitoring:** Can gather samples, monitor water quality, or operate sensors remotely.
    

---

### **Control & Containment**

- Optional **neural implant** allows UL monitoring of location, stress levels, and emergency extraction.
    
- Must be trained to balance underwater instincts with land-based tasks.
    
- Nutritional adaptations accommodate both land and water consumption.
    

---

### **Psychological Notes**

- Initial sensation of gills and tail may cause anxiety or claustrophobia.
    
- Bioluminescence can trigger self-consciousness; training programs available.
    
- Subjects typically develop a strong affinity for aquatic environments.
