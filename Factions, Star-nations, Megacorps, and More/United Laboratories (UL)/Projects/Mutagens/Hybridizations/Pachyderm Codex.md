### **Overview**

Project: Pachyderm Codex introduces **pachyderm-derived traits** into human and animal hosts, producing **massive, resilient siege organisms**. Through **foodborne genetic modifiers**, subjects undergo rapid skeletal reinforcement, dermal thickening, and muscular hypertrophy.

The result is a towering hybrid: less agile, but nearly indestructible in direct combat. These hybrids instinctively form **herds** and can be directed via **UL neural control signals**, making them ideal for both **siege warfare** and **labor deployment** in hostile or resource-scarce environments.

### **Tactical Applications**

- **Siege Warfare:** Breaks through fortifications and urban defenses.
    
- **Shock and Awe:** Sheer size and power demoralize populations.
    
- **Labor Deployment:** Can be harnessed for large-scale construction or demolition.
    
- **Zone Domination:** Herd behavior makes them effective at holding ground en masse.
    

---

### **Containment Protocols**

- Agility loss makes them vulnerable to high-speed aerial or precision strikes.
    
- Herding instinct means they follow a dominant leader‚Äî**control that leader, and the herd follows**.
    
- Excessive hunger makes them dependent on UL-supplied rations, a built-in control method.
    
- Neural pattern embedding ensures UL override, but corruption of signals can lead to rampaging behavior.
    

---

### **Operational Warnings**

- Host body mass increases may rupture pre-existing weak cardiovascular systems.
    
- If not properly controlled, Pachyderms may cause **indiscriminate destruction**, including UL assets.
    
- Herd stampedes can destabilize entire cities if directed without precision.
