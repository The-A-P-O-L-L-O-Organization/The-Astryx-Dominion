### **United Laboratories Facility Dossier — HADAL-Ω (Next-Generation Sub-Menagerie)**

**Designation:** HADAL-Ω (Omega)  
**Location:** Ultra-Deep Hadal Trench Z-19, UL Sector O-7, Exoplanet Omega-5  
**Operational Status:** Fully Active  
**Function:** Advanced mutagen research, hybrid creation, containment, and behavioral study  
**Date Operational:** ██████████

---

### **Facility Overview:**

**Purpose:**

- HADAL-Ω is a **state-of-the-art, large-scale HADAL research hub** designed to replace all earlier sub-menagerie facilities.
    
- Capable of producing, testing, and containing **highly complex aquatic and hybrid subjects** under extreme pressures and variable environmental simulations.
    
- Integrates **next-generation mutagenic synthesis**, AI-controlled monitoring, automated containment, and adaptive security systems.
    

**Capacity & Scale:**

- Total Facility Size: ~14× the volume of Alpha-12, spanning multiple cylindrical and dome modules connected by reinforced corridors.
    
- Containment: 320+ specialized tanks for humanoid, hybrid, and experimental aquatic subjects.
    
- Laboratories: 32 research labs including genetic synthesis, mutagen refinement, behavioral analysis, and emergency experimentation units.
    
- Storage: 48 secure vaults for mutagens, equipment, and research data.
    
- Observation & Control Hubs: 6 centralized hubs with panoramic trench views, holographic displays, and AI-assisted monitoring.
    
- Emergency & Environmental Chambers: Full-scale simulations of deep-sea, high-pressure, and hybrid-specific habitats.
    

---

### **Architecture & Design:**

- **Modules:** Cylindrical, dome-shaped, and irregular pressure-adaptive chambers for extreme stress tests; connected by high-pressure corridors and reinforced tunnels.
    
- **Hull & Exterior:** Ultra-dense alloys and adaptive polymers; external stealth coating reduces sonar and visual detection; bioluminescent panels provide low-level ambient lighting for external observation.
    
- **Docking & Logistics:** Multiple pressurized docking bays for cargo, submersibles, and personnel; mechanized cranes and magnetic rails handle specimens and experimental materials.
    
- **Interior Systems:**
    
    - Fully automated containment and transport systems for mutagens and specimens.
        
    - AI-assisted monitoring for behavioral and physiological metrics.
        
    - Self-sustaining life-support systems capable of independent operation for years if cut off from UL command.
        

---

### **Security & Containment:**

- **Active Systems:**
    
    - Automated drone patrols equipped with non-lethal and lethal countermeasures.
        
    - AI-controlled environmental locks to isolate breached areas.
        
    - Pressure and motion sensors across all corridors and chambers.
        
- **Containment Integrity:**
    
    - Tanks adapt fluid composition, pressure, and temperature dynamically to subject physiology.
        
    - Biome simulation chambers for advanced behavioral conditioning and mutagen testing.
        
- **Self-Destruct & Emergency Protocols:**
    
    - Multi-layered emergency failsafes; only remotely activated after transport of all specimens, data, and mutagens.
        

---

### **Operational Features:**

1. **AI Integration:**
    
    - AI manages environmental conditions, subject health metrics, mutagen synthesis schedules, and security simultaneously.
        
    - Real-time predictive modeling allows preemptive containment of potential breaches or subject instability.
        
2. **Hybrid Specialization:**
    
    - Tanks and habitats are modular, accommodating **extra limbs, unique respiratory systems, and adaptive pressure tolerance**.
        
    - Allows for more dangerous or complex hybrids than the old Alpha-12 site.
        
3. **Data & Mutagen Management:**
    
    - All research data and mutagen samples are automatically cataloged, backed up in triple-redundant vaults, and transported via automated systems to secure areas.
        
4. **Research Expansion Potential:**
    
    - Facility has modular expansion zones; new containment, laboratory, or environmental simulation chambers can be added without shutting down active operations.

### **Operational Notes:**

- Designed to **handle high-risk, high-value experiments** with minimal human oversight.
    
- UL considers HADAL-Ω the **gold standard for mutagenic research**, integrating lessons from Alpha-12 and other abandoned facilities.
    
- The facility is intended for **long-term containment, research, and production**, with scalability for larger operations across the Hadal Trench network.
    

---

**Conclusion:**  
HADAL-Ω represents a **massive leap in deep-sea research capacity and security**. Its scale, automation, and specialized design make it an ideal successor to the abandoned Alpha-12 site. The contrast between the two emphasizes the evolution of UL operations: Alpha-12 is a discarded relic of early experimentation, while HADAL-Ω is a near-fortress of controlled mutagenic and hybrid research, capable of housing and managing hundreds of dangerous experimental subjects simultaneously.
