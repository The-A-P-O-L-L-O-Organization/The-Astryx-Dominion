## **Planetary Profile: Jurelia**

**Designation:** Arctis IV  
**System:** Arctis Prime  
**Orbital Distance:** 5.6 AU  
**Type:** Gas giant  
**Coloration:** Striking orange-brown with high-contrast wind bands  
**Gravity (cloud tops):** 2.3 G (surface undefined; no solid surface)  
**Atmosphere:** Predominantly hydrogen and helium; trace methane, ammonia, and neon  
**Special Resource:** Helium-3 deposits in upper cloud layers, highly valuable for fusion energy research

---

## **Atmospheric Features**

- **High-speed wind bands:** Jets reaching 1,200 km/h along latitudinal stripes
    
- **Storm systems:** Persistent, long-lasting cyclones that circulate for centuries in some cases
    
- **Cloud layers:** Differentiated by altitude and chemical composition; orange-brown bands rich in particulates for spectroscopy studies
    
- **Aurora phenomena:** Generated by magnetic field interactions with solar wind; used for magnetosphere research
    

---

## **UL Infrastructure & Research**

### **Floating Orbital Platforms**

- **Purpose:** Monitoring atmospheric dynamics, harvesting Helium-3, and deploying research drones
    
- **Design:** Semi-submersible platforms that float in the upper cloud layers using anti-gravity stabilization and helium-3 buoyancy pockets
    
- **Facilities:** Laboratories, fusion energy testing units, cloud-sampling drones, and advanced sensor arrays
    

### **Cloud-Penetration Drones**

- Autonomous drones designed to navigate turbulent upper and lower atmospheric bands
    
- Equipped with adaptive hulls to withstand extreme pressures and wind shear
    
- Collect real-time atmospheric chemistry, temperature, and wind data for UL research
    

---

## **Moons of Jurelia**

### 1. **Thalmar**

- Volcanically active with molten lava lakes and continuous geysers
    
- UL mining operations extract rare minerals and study planetary volcanology
    
- Several underground UL research bunkers monitor geophysical activity
    

### 2. **Nivessa**

- Ice-capped and highly reflective; excellent for optical and telescope arrays
    
- Houses long-range deep-space observatories, magnetosphere scanners, and exoplanet survey stations
    

### 3. **Corvyn**

- Small, rocky, with negligible atmosphere
    
- Zero-gravity combat training simulations for UL military and security personnel
    
- Equipped with modular barracks, drone ranges, and tactical simulation hubs
    

### 4. **Althira**

- Slightly larger rocky-ice hybrid moon with dense upper atmosphere
    
- Aerodynamics testing and flight research for sub-orbital and atmospheric vehicles
    
- UL has experimental wind tunnels embedded into natural canyon formations for high-G maneuvers
