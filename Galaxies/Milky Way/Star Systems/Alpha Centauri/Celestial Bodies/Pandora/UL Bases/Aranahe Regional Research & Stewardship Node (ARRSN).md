## Site Philosophy & Placement
- **Location:** Sited at the periphery of core Aranahe territory on the edge of a cleared ridge overlooking the Kinglor/Clouded Forest transition — chosen to be visible (trust-building) yet outside sacred groves and songline corridors.
- **Design Ethos:** **Transparent Reciprocity** — the base emphasizes openness, co-stewardship, and forest-regenerative architecture that visibly assists local ecology without obstructing ritual or migration routes.
- **Cultural Considerations:** Full pre-deployment consultation with Aranahe elders and shamen; ceremonial blessing performed. Aranahe co-signatory on charter and co-management agreements.

---

## Architecture & Layout
### 1. Stewardship Atrium (SA)
- **Function:** Public-facing heart of the base; council chamber for UL–Aranahe meetings, education, and cultural exchange.
- **Design:** Elevated timber-lattice platform woven with living vines and biolum benches; acoustically designed for songline performance and translated projection.
- **Features:** Dual-key access consoles, ritual alcove reserved for Aranahe use, public research exhibits celebrating local flora/fauna.

### 2. Ethno-Botanical Research Wing (EBRW)
- **Function:** Primary research on forest regrowth, mycorrhizal networks, seed banks, and Pantheon Genome-compatible gene modules derived from Aranahe plant species.
- **Facilities:** sealed wet labs (non-invasive sampling protocols), seed & microbiome vault (dual-key), controlled growth domes for restoration trials.
- **Integration:** Aranahe co-research stations and apprenticeships embedded in labs; sample extraction only with clan consent.

### 3. Canopy Observation & Skyfarm (COS)
- **Function:** Observation platforms and regenerative plantings; serves as canopy access for Skyfang field trials and reforestation work.
- **Design:** Suspended walkways & soft-tethered platforms; minimal weight footprint; integrated pollinator habitats.
- **Use:** joint UL–Aranahe canopy patrols, seed dispersal sorties, and Skyfang training corridors.

### 4. Field Repair & Restoration Depot (FRRD)
- **Function:** Staging for restoration drones, soft-robot tenders, soil bioreactors, and phytoremediation units.
- **Design:** Open-air bays with non-invasive ground anchors; drone docking includes biofilters to prevent cross-contamination.

### 5. Community & Training Quarter (CTQ)
- **Function:** Living quarters for visiting Aranahe researchers, UL apprentices, and cultural exchange programs.
- **Design:** Communal longhouses built with local materials (co-created with clan artisans), sleeping platforms, shared kitchens, and education rooms.

### 6. Flux & Environmental Stability Hub (FESH)
- **Function:** Magnetic flux anchors, environmental sensors, GAIA nodes, and early-warning arrays for storms and bioelectric events.
- **Design:** Low-profile anchor arrays distributed to avoid root interference; flux-locked comms beacons for local stabilization.

## Operational Systems & Protocols
### Research & Data Governance
- **Dual-Key Stewardship:** all biological samples and cultural datasets stored under dual-key (Aranahe elder + UL director) control; no transfer off-moon without joint authorization and GC audit.
- **Songline Preservation Protocol:** when digitizing songlines or rituals for research, ritual context and performance metadata must be included; raw data may be sealed with Aranahe keys.
- **Open Lab Days:** scheduled periods where clan members observe, contribute, and co-author research outputs.

### Restoration & Fieldwork
- **Reforestation Campaigns:** co-designed planting schedules based on Aranahe seasonal knowledge and GAIA model projections.
- **Skyfang Dispersion:** Skyfangs used for seed dispersal under Aranahe co-pilot guidance; flight corridors mapped with clan input.
- **Bioreactor Use:** soil detox and microbial amendments deployed in increments with continuous monitoring and Aranahe approval.

### Environmental & Cultural Safeguards
- **Sacred Corridor Mapping:** base maps and operations avoid all mapped sacred nodes and songline corridors; any needed access requires ritual invitation.
- **Acoustic & Light Limits:** external emissions constrained to culturally safe bands and intensities agreed upon by the council.
- **Public Access:** Aranahe may access Stewardship Atrium freely for rituals and education; UL staff attend only upon invitation.
